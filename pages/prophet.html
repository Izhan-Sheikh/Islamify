The Life of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH)

A mercy to all mankind

Introduction

Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) was born in Makkah in the year 570 CE. He was the final messenger sent by Allah to guide humanity, completing the long line of prophets that began with Adam.

Surah Al-Anbiya, Verse 107
وَمَا أَرْسَلْنَاكَ إِلَّا رَحْمَةً لِّلْعَالَمِينَ
"And We have not sent you, [O Muhammad], except as a mercy to the worlds."
570 CE

Birth and Early Life

Born in Makkah on the 12th of Rabi' al-Awwal, in the Year of the Elephant. His father Abdullah passed away before his birth, and his mother Aminah died when he was six. Raised by his grandfather Abdul-Muttalib and later by his uncle Abu Talib. Known for his honesty and trustworthiness, earning the title "Al-Amin" (The Trustworthy).

595 CE

Marriage to Khadijah

Married Khadijah bint Khuwaylid, a wealthy and respected businesswoman. Their marriage lasted 25 years until her death. She was the first to believe in his prophethood and provided him with unwavering support.

610 CE

First Revelation

Received the first revelation in the Cave of Hira during the month of Ramadan. The first verses revealed were from Surah Al-Alaq:

Surah Al-Alaq, Verse 1-5
اقْرَأْ بِاسْمِ رَبِّكَ الَّذِي خَلَقَ * خَلَقَ الْإِنسَانَ مِنْ عَلَقٍ * اقْرَأْ وَرَبُّكَ الْأَكْرَمُ * الَّذِي عَلَّمَ بِالْقَلَمِ * عَلَّمَ الْإِنسَانَ مَا لَمْ يَعْلَمْ
"Read in the name of your Lord who created * Created man from a clinging substance * Read, and your Lord is the most Generous * Who taught by the pen * Taught man that which he knew not."
613 CE

Public Declaration

Began publicly preaching Islam after three years of private invitation. Faced increasing opposition from the Quraysh leaders who saw Islam as a threat to their power and traditions.

615 CE

Migration to Abyssinia

Some of the early Muslims migrated to Abyssinia (modern-day Ethiopia) to escape persecution. The Christian king, Negus, granted them protection after hearing the Quran's description of Jesus.

619 CE

Year of Sorrow

Lost his beloved wife Khadijah and his uncle Abu Talib, who had been his protector. This year is known as the "Year of Sorrow" due to these significant losses.

620 CE

Night Journey and Ascension

Experienced the miraculous Night Journey (Isra) from Makkah to Jerusalem and the Ascension (Mi'raj) to the heavens. This event is mentioned in the Quran:

Surah Al-Isra, Verse 1
سُبْحَانَ الَّذِي أَسْرَىٰ بِعَبْدِهِ لَيْلًا مِّنَ الْمَسْجِدِ الْحَرَامِ إِلَى الْمَسْجِدِ الْأَقْصَى الَّذِي بَارَكْنَا حَوْلَهُ لِنُرِيَهُ مِنْ آيَاتِنَا ۚ إِنَّهُ هُوَ السَّمِيعُ الْبَصِيرُ
"Exalted is He who took His Servant by night from al-Masjid al-Haram to al-Masjid al-Aqsa, whose surroundings We have blessed, to show him of Our signs. Indeed, He is the Hearing, the Seeing."
622 CE

Migration to Madinah

Faced increasing persecution in Makkah, leading to the Hijrah (migration) to Madinah. This event marks the beginning of the Islamic calendar. Established the first Islamic state and built the first mosque in Madinah.

624 CE

Battle of Badr

The first major battle between the Muslims and the Quraysh. Despite being outnumbered, the Muslims achieved a decisive victory. This battle is mentioned in the Quran:

Surah Al-Imran, Verse 123
وَلَقَدْ نَصَرَكُمُ اللَّهُ بِبَدْرٍ وَأَنتُمْ أَذِلَّةٌ ۖ فَاتَّقُوا اللَّهَ لَعَلَّكُمْ تَشْكُرُونَ
"And Allah had already given you victory at [the battle of] Badr, while you were few in number. Then fear Allah; perhaps you will be grateful."
625 CE

Battle of Uhud

A challenging battle where the Muslims faced setbacks but learned important lessons about discipline and obedience. The Prophet was injured, but the Muslims maintained their faith.

627 CE

Battle of the Trench

Defended Madinah against a large coalition of tribes. The Muslims dug a trench around the city, which proved to be an effective defensive strategy. The siege ended without major fighting.

628 CE

Treaty of Hudaybiyyah

Signed a peace treaty with the Quraysh, which initially seemed disadvantageous but later proved to be a strategic victory. This period allowed Islam to spread more widely.

630 CE

Conquest of Makkah

Returned to Makkah in triumph, showing mercy and forgiveness to those who had persecuted him and his followers. Cleansed the Kaaba of idols and established it as a house of worship for Allah alone.

632 CE

Farewell Pilgrimage

Performed his final Hajj and delivered his farewell sermon, emphasizing the equality of all people and the importance of following the Quran and his Sunnah. Passed away shortly after in Madinah.

His Character

Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) was known for his excellent character, mercy, and wisdom. His wife Aisha described him as "a walking Quran" - his life was the perfect embodiment of the Quran's teachings. He was known for his:

  • Honesty and trustworthiness (Al-Amin)
  • Mercy and compassion
  • Justice and fairness
  • Humility and simplicity
  • Patience and perseverance
  • Generosity and kindness
Surah Al-Qalam, Verse 4
وَإِنَّكَ لَعَلَىٰ خُلُقٍ عَظِيمٍ
"And indeed, you are of a great moral character."

His Family Life

The Prophet's family life provides excellent examples of how to maintain strong family bonds and treat family members with kindness and respect. He was:

  • A loving husband to his wives
  • A caring father to his children
  • A respectful son-in-law
  • A kind grandfather
  • A supportive uncle

His Leadership

As a leader, the Prophet demonstrated exceptional qualities that continue to inspire leaders today:

  • Consultation (Shura) with his companions
  • Justice and equality for all
  • Mercy and forgiveness
  • Strategic thinking and planning
  • Leading by example

His Legacy

The Prophet's life and teachings continue to guide millions of Muslims worldwide. His Sunnah (way of life) provides practical examples of how to implement the Quran's teachings in daily life. His legacy includes:

  • The preservation and spread of the Quran
  • The establishment of a just society
  • The promotion of knowledge and education
  • The emphasis on human rights and dignity
  • The foundation of Islamic civilization
Surah Al-Ahzab, Verse 21
لَّقَدْ كَانَ لَكُمْ فِي رَسُولِ اللَّهِ أُسْوَةٌ حَسَنَةٌ لِّمَن كَانَ يَرْجُو اللَّهَ وَالْيَوْمَ الْآخِرَ وَذَكَرَ اللَّهَ كَثِيرًا
"There has certainly been for you in the Messenger of Allah an excellent pattern for anyone whose hope is in Allah and the Last Day and [who] remembers Allah often."
