The Story of Prophet Musa

A journey of faith, courage, and divine guidance

Introduction

The story of Prophet Musa (Moses) is one of the most frequently mentioned stories in the Quran. It's a story of divine intervention, faith, and the struggle against oppression.

Surah Al-Qasas, Verse 3
نَتْلُو عَلَيْكَ مِن نَّبَإِ مُوسَىٰ وَفِرْعَوْنَ بِالْحَقِّ لِقَوْمٍ يُؤْمِنُونَ
"We recite to you from the news of Moses and Pharaoh in truth for a people who believe."

Birth and Early Life

Musa was born during a time when Pharaoh was killing all male children of the Children of Israel. His mother, following divine inspiration, placed him in a basket and set it adrift on the Nile River.

Surah Al-Qasas, Verse 7
وَأَوْحَيْنَا إِلَىٰ أُمِّ مُوسَىٰ أَنْ أَرْضِعِيهِ ۖ فَإِذَا خِفْتِ عَلَيْهِ فَأَلْقِيهِ فِي الْيَمِّ وَلَا تَخَافِي وَلَا تَحْزَنِي ۖ إِنَّا رَادُّوهُ إِلَيْكِ وَجَاعِلُوهُ مِنَ الْمُرْسَلِينَ
"And We inspired to the mother of Moses, 'Suckle him; but when you fear for him, cast him into the river and do not fear and do not grieve. Indeed, We will return him to you and will make him [one] of the messengers.'"

Growing Up in Pharaoh's Palace

Musa was found by Pharaoh's family and raised in the palace. Despite growing up in luxury, he never forgot his true identity and always stood for justice.

Surah Al-Qasas, Verse 14
وَلَمَّا بَلَغَ أَشُدَّهُ وَاسْتَوَىٰ آتَيْنَاهُ حُكْمًا وَعِلْمًا ۚ وَكَذَٰلِكَ نَجْزِي الْمُحْسِنِينَ
"And when he attained his full strength and was [mentally] mature, We bestowed upon him judgement and knowledge. And thus do We reward the doers of good."

Flight to Madyan

After accidentally killing an Egyptian, Musa fled to Madyan where he helped two women water their flock and was eventually married to one of them.

Surah Al-Qasas, Verse 28
قَالَ إِنِّي أُرِيدُ أَنْ أُنكِحَكَ إِحْدَى ابْنَتَيَّ هَاتَيْنِ عَلَىٰ أَن تَأْجُرَنِي ثَمَانِيَ حِجَجٍ ۖ فَإِنْ أَتْمَمْتَ عَشْرًا فَمِنْ عِندِكَ ۖ وَمَا أُرِيدُ أَنْ أَشُقَّ عَلَيْكَ ۚ سَتَجِدُنِي إِن شَاءَ اللَّهُ مِنَ الصَّالِحِينَ
"He said, 'Indeed, I wish to wed you one of these, my two daughters, on [the condition] that you serve me for eight years; but if you complete ten, it will be [as a favor] from you. And I do not wish to put you in difficulty. You will find me, if Allah wills, from the righteous.'"

Prophethood and Confrontation with Pharaoh

Musa was chosen by Allah to deliver His message to Pharaoh and free the Children of Israel from oppression. With his brother Harun (Aaron) by his side, he performed miracles and called Pharaoh to the truth.

Surah Ta-Ha, Verse 47
فَأْتِيَاهُ فَقُولَا إِنَّا رَسُولَا رَبِّكَ فَأَرْسِلْ مَعَنَا بَنِي إِسْرَائِيلَ وَلَا تُعَذِّبْهُمْ ۖ قَدْ جِئْنَاكَ بِآيَةٍ مِّن رَّبِّكَ ۖ وَالسَّلَامُ عَلَىٰ مَنِ اتَّبَعَ الْهُدَىٰ
"So go to him and say, 'Indeed, we are messengers of your Lord, so send with us the Children of Israel and do not torment them. We have come to you with a sign from your Lord. And peace will be upon he who follows the guidance.'"

The Exodus and Crossing the Sea

After Pharaoh refused to believe, Allah guided Musa and the Children of Israel to escape. When Pharaoh pursued them, Allah parted the sea, allowing them to cross safely while drowning Pharaoh and his army.

Surah Ash-Shu'ara, Verse 63
فَأَوْحَيْنَا إِلَىٰ مُوسَىٰ أَنِ اضْرِب بِّعَصَاكَ الْبَحْرَ ۖ فَانفَلَقَ فَكَانَ كُلُّ فِرْقٍ كَالطَّوْدِ الْعَظِيمِ
"Then We inspired to Moses, 'Strike with your staff the sea,' and it parted, and each portion was like a great towering mountain."
